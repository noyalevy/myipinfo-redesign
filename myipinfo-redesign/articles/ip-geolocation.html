IP Geolocation: How Websites Know Where You Are

📅 January 2025⏱ 6 min read✍️ MyIPInfo Team

When you visit a website, it often already knows your country, city, and language — before you tell it anything. This is IP geolocation at work. Here's exactly how it works, how accurate it is, and what you can do about it.

How IP Geolocation Works

IP geolocation works by looking up your IP address in a geolocation database — a massive, continuously updated mapping of IP address ranges to geographic locations.

These databases are built from multiple sources:

  • Regional Internet Registries (RIRs) — Organizations like ARIN (Americas), RIPE (Europe), and APNIC (Asia-Pacific) publish which IP blocks are allocated to which countries and ISPs
  • ISP data — Some ISPs share where they deploy their IP addresses
  • Active measurements — Network latency measurements help triangulate approximate locations
  • User corrections — Some providers allow users to submit corrections when their IP location is wrong

Major geolocation database providers include MaxMind (GeoIP2), ip-api.com, and ipapi.co. Websites query these databases in real-time using your IP address.

⚠️ Common Misconception

Your IP address cannot reveal your home address or street location. Anyone who claims to have pinpointed your exact house from an IP address is either lying or has obtained data through other means (such as a legal subpoena to your ISP).

How Websites Use Your Location

  • Content localization — Auto-detecting your language and currency
  • Geo-restrictions — Blocking content not licensed in your region (Netflix, Spotify)
  • Fraud detection — Flagging logins from unexpected countries
  • Targeted advertising — Showing local ads and deals
  • Compliance — GDPR cookie banners for EU users, age verification
  • CDN routing — Serving content from the nearest server for speed
  • Tax calculation — Applying the correct VAT or sales tax rate

What IP Geolocation Cannot Reveal

Despite what some claim online, an IP address alone cannot reveal:

  • Your exact home or work address
  • Your name or identity
  • Your browsing history or activity
  • Personal information about you

This information is only accessible to your ISP and law enforcement with proper legal authorization (subpoena or court order).

How to Protect Your Location Privacy

  • VPN — Routes traffic through a server in another location, replacing your IP with the VPN server's IP and location
  • Tor Browser — Routes through multiple relays, each seeing only the previous hop's IP
  • Proxy server — Similar to VPN but typically without encryption

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does my IP show the wrong city? +
IP geolocation databases aren't perfect. Your IP might be registered to your ISP's headquarters city rather than where you physically are. ISPs own large blocks of IPs and don't always update databases when reassigning addresses. Errors of 50–200 km are common at the city level.
Can a website see my GPS location from my IP? +
No. IP geolocation is an approximation based on database lookups — it does not access your GPS. If a website asks for your precise location, it uses the browser's Geolocation API, which requires your explicit permission and accesses your device's GPS, Wi-Fi triangulation, or cell tower data.
Can I request correction of my IP geolocation data? +
Yes. Major geolocation providers like MaxMind offer correction forms. However, the underlying issue is usually how your ISP has registered its IP blocks with regional registries — which is harder to change. A VPN is a more reliable solution for appearing in a different location.
